BUSHPOPPY
Dendromecon rigidum Bentham
The profuse bright yellow flowers of bushpoppy give the shrub a striking appearance. It grows from two to eight feet high, and blooms almost throughout the year. The leaves are willowlike and leathery in texture, and the main stems are rich brown in color.
This member of the Poppy Family has a restricted range in southern California and northern lower California.
